The problem

Crypto payments assume connectivity.
The world doesn't.

Every crypto payment system today requires an active internet connection to function. That excludes billions of merchants and customers across emerging markets.

Always-on connectivity required
Traditional crypto wallets and payment terminals need a live RPC connection to sign, broadcast, and confirm every transaction in real time.
$5.5T market locked out
Street markets in Lagos, rural traders in Southeast Asia, and mobile vendors across Africa cannot adopt crypto because their connectivity is intermittent.
No offline-safe alternative
Existing offline workarounds — paper wallets, hardware signers — offer no fraud protection, no receipt trail, and no path to on-chain settlement.
How it works

Four steps. Zero internet required at point of sale.

1
Merchant opens an offline session
Before going offline, the merchant commits a Merkle root of pre-generated nonces to the NonceRegistry contract on HashKey Chain. This creates a cryptographic session with up to 1,000 payment slots.
2
Customer scans QR and signs offline
The customer's wallet scans the merchant's QR code and signs a PaymentIntent using EIP-712 typed data — entirely offline. No blockchain call is made. The signature is verified locally by the merchant device.
3
Intents queue in local storage
Signed intents persist in IndexedDB across app restarts. The merchant continues accepting payments. Each intent consumes one pre-committed nonce, making double-spend mathematically impossible.
4
Connectivity returns — auto batch settlement
When the device reconnects, BatchSettler submits all queued intents in a single transaction to HashPointEscrow on HashKey Chain. Funds transfer atomically. HSP receipts are emitted for every payment.

Features

Everything a merchant needs

HashPoint is a complete stack — from cryptographic session management to PWA merchant terminal to gas-efficient batch settlement.

Merkle anti-double-spend
Before going offline, merchants commit a Merkle root of pre-generated nonces to the NonceRegistry contract. Each payment consumes one nonce provably, making replay attacks impossible even with no real-time chain access.
EIP-712 offline signing
PaymentIntents are structured as EIP-712 typed data. Customers sign with any compatible wallet — no internet required. Merchants verify the signature locally in milliseconds before accepting payment.
Persistent intent queue
All signed intents are stored in IndexedDB, surviving app restarts, device reboots, and extended offline periods. Nothing is lost. Intents automatically submit when connectivity returns.
HSP-native settlement
HashPoint uses HSP (HashKey Settlement Protocol) as the payment messaging layer. Every settlement emits HSP PAYMENT_REQUEST, PAYMENT_CONFIRMATION, and PAYMENT_RECEIPT messages — fully indexed and composable.
Gas relay service
Merchants who lack ETH for gas can delegate settlement to the HashPoint relay. The relay batches intents from multiple merchants, deducts a 0.05% relay fee, and submits on their behalf — no gas wallet needed.
PWA merchant terminal
A Progressive Web App that runs on any Android phone. Works offline. Installable with one tap. Session management, QR payment generation, settlement queue, and real-time sync status — all in a single app.
